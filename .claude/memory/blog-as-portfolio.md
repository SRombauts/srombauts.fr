The srombauts.eu blog doubles as a professional portfolio: alongside the howto/tutorial
posts, it serves as durable, verifiable evidence of past work that backs the LinkedIn
profile and CV. This raises the quality bar: posts should be substantive and credible,
not filler.

Each retrospective should also work as a reference *for the author himself*: part
nostalgia, part a recall aid. So a good post is not just a showcase ("here is a repo");
it should capture the why: the problem it solved, the key technical decisions and
trade-offs, and what he learned or would do differently. Keep that public-appropriate,
but make the reasoning easy to recover on a re-read.

Priority backlog (mostly from the portfolio TODO and pre-blog career):
- GitHub portfolio repos that lack a post yet (ecs, SDL, OpenGL/gltext, Ogre3D, ZMQCpp,
  and the C++ libs). Dates and screenshots are recoverable from each repo.
- Pre-blog history: discovering CVS at INEO Systrans (2003), switching to Subversion
  (~2005) incl. team workflow/docs; Unreal community mini-demos; early-career FPGA /
  hardware description languages.

**Why:** changes what "good" means here. The audience includes technical readers and
hiring managers, so honesty about dating and avoiding fabricated specifics matter more
than post count.

**How to apply:** Do not silently backdate posts before the blog's first real post
(2010-09-07) since the blog did not exist then; use a clear retrospective frame for
pre-2010 material. Verify technical claims against the actual repos; ask Sébastien for
specifics on INEO Systrans / FPGA work rather than inventing them, and avoid anything
that could breach a past employer's confidentiality. Keep employer references historical
and neutral ([[blog-employer-framing]]).
